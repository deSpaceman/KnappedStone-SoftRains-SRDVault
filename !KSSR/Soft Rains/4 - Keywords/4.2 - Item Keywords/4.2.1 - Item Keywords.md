Here is a list of base system Item Keywords, divided in categories by Item Type. The top most part of each category being Notable Keywords, either due to being shared by multiple items of that type or coming into play often, underneath are General Keywords that can be applied to that Item Type.

Both lists are presented in alphabetical order. Further separations will be made for the sake of convenience.

# General Equipment Keywords
Keywords shared by all Equipment Items.

### General Equipment Notable Keywords
**Broken**: This piece of equipment is broken. It has no effects and no uses until it is repaired.

**Damaged X**: This piece of equipment has been damaged due to Mishaps or the actions of a Creature or time. Reduce the Power of this equipment by the number X specified in this Keyword, if the Power reaches or drops bellow 0, remove this Keyword and give the item the Broken Keyword. Removing Damaged X Keyword can be done via a Mind Roll with difficulty equal to the amount of Damaged X you want removed, you must also have the appropriate materials to repair the item.

### General Equipment General Keywords
**Concealable**: It is easy to hide this piece of equipment. Tests to detect this item when hidden have Disadvantage 2.

**Handed, One**: This item can only be used with one hand.

**Handed, Two**: This item can only be used with two hands.

**Handed, Four**: This item can only be used with four hands.

**Inert**: This item cannot be affected by magic.

**Makeshift**: This piece of equipment was made in a pinch. If it breaks (I.E.: receives the Broken Keyword), it won't be repairable.

**Reinforced**: If this piece of equipment can take the Damaged Keyword one extra time.

**Reliable**: This piece of equipment does not suffer Mishaps if a roll using it succeeds.

**Versatile**: This item can be used with one hand or two hands. If the user chooses to do the latter, raise the primary Power of this piece of equipment by 1.

# Tool Keywords
The Keywords pertaining to Tools. It inherits all the Keywords in the General Equipment category.

### Tool Notable Keywords
**Base Tool**: This tool is not an actual item, it is instead the baseline stats for this kind of tool. To turn it into an actual item, add the Keywords between () from the appropriate materials to this Base Tool's Keywords.

**Tool Power X**: This tool has X of (Tool) Power, which reduces the amount of time needed to execute an action that involves the specific power and may allow to do actions that need higher amounts of power. For example, a tool with Mining Power 2 will take less time to mine Stone and can mine Sturdy Ore. Check the Power Reference List for some examples.

**Weapon**: This tool can also act as a weapon. Unless stated otherwise, turn its Tool Power into Physical Power when attacking. This also allows the Tool to inherit Weapon Keywords.

### Tool General Keywords
**Multitool Power X**: This tool is capable of executing the activities of two or more tools at Tool Power X. The tools this multitool can assume the purpose of will be listed between [ ].

**Repair Tool**: This tool can be used to repair Structures, Vehicles and items with the Damaged X or Broken Keywords without the need to roll.

# Weapon Keywords
The Keywords pertaining to Weapons. It inherits all the Keywords in the General Equipment category.

### Weapon Notable Keywords
**Base Weapon**: This weapon is not an actual item, it is instead the baseline stats for this kind of weapon. To turn it into an actual item, add the Keywords between () from the appropriate materials to this Base Weapon's Keywords.

**Weapon Power X**: This weapon adds X amount of (Weapon) damage to the attack. For example, a weapon with Physical Power 2 adds 2 Physical Damage, while a weapon with Fire Power 4 adds 4 Fire Damage. Check the Power Reference List for some examples.

### Weapon Range Keywords
**Area YxY**: This weapon attacks in a YxY square area. Unless stated otherwise, the origin of the square area must be adjacent to the user. For a better frame of reference, check the diagram under this description.

![[Area Diagram.png|800x560]]

**Aura YxY**: This weapon attacks in a YxY square aura around the user. Y must always be an odd number (E.G.: 3x3, 5x5, 7x7...), and the center of the aura is not affected by the attack, unless stated otherwise. For a better frame of reference, check the diagram under this description.

![[Aura Diagram.png|560x640]]

**Cone X**: This weapon attacks in a cone shape that covers X+1 height and 2X+1 base if done horizontally and vertically, or X height and X+1 base if done diagonally. For a better frame of reference, check the diagram under this description.

![[Cone Diagram.png|720x480]]

**Line X**: This weapon attacks in a vertical line that is X+1 Gridspaces long. If the line is diagonal, its length is X Gridspaces. Unless stated otherwise, the attack stops at the first obstacle it hits. For a better frame of reference, check the diagrams under this description.

![[Line Diagram.png|1120x720]]

**Range**: This weapon is a ranged weapon, this means a few things:
> Their Weapon Power is only accessible by shooting a projectile using this weapon. If it is used for a melee attack, they will only provide Physical Power 1;
> Unless stated otherwise, shooting a projectile at an adjacent target provides Disadvantage 4;
> Unless stated otherwise, having a non-obscuring obstacle (E.G.: a Creature) on the way of your target provides Disadvantage 2;
> Unless stated otherwise, the projectile stops at the first obscuring obstacle (E.G.: a wall) it encounters or at the end of its range;
> If this weapon or the shot projectile has the Keyword Area, Cone, Line, or Aura, you may choose any point within the range of this weapon to serve as the point the origin of the given shape.

The reach of the weapon is dictated by the modifier on the Range Keyword:
- Lesser Range: This weapon reaches 15 Gridspaces in range;
- Greater Range: This weapon reaches 45 Gridspaces in range;
- Extreme Range: This weapon reaches 150 Gridspaces in range, but it has Disadvantage 4 on hitting targets up to 30 Gridspaces of distance.

**Reach**: Melee attacks done by this weapon can reach an additional Gridspace.

**Sweeping**: Melee attacks done by this weapon can cover a 3 Gridspace horizontal line whose center is the space right in front of the user. If done as a diagonal attack, it will take the shape of an L, with the corner being the space diagonal to the user. For a better frame of reference, check the diagrams under this description.

![[Sweeping Diagram.png|720x480]]

### Weapon General Keywords
**Anti-Vehicle**: This weapon deals double damage to Creatures, full damage to Vehicles, and half damage to Structures.

**Armor Shatter**: On Criticals, the user of this weapon may decide to inflict the Damaged X Keyword to a target's armor.

**Bleeding**: On attack rolls that surpasses the target's defense roll by 2 Successes, this weapon's attack also afflicts the target with the Bleeding Condition.

**Crewkiller**: This Weapon does not suffer Disadvantage when attacking the Crew of a Vehicle.

**Freezing**: On Criticals, this weapon's attack also afflicts the target with the Frozen Condition.

**Grabbing**: On attack rolls that surpasses the Target's Defense Roll by 2 Successes, this weapon's attack Grapples the target. Grappling is described in more detail in the [[5.4.2 - Maneuvers|Maneuvers]] section of Knapped Stone.

**Guts**: The sheer power, reach, or brutality of this weapon guarantees it to damage targets even on glancing hits. If an attack using a weapon with this keyword misses, deal damage according to the modifier on the Guts Keyword.
- Lesser Guts: The weapon deals 1 point of damage, respecting their type of Weapon Power (E.G.: Physical Power);
- Guts: The weapon deals half their Weapon Power (E.G.: Physical Power), rounded up;
- Greater Guts: The weapon deals their full Weapon Power (E.G.: Physical Power).

**High Caliber**: On Criticals, this weapon ignores the target's Shielding Power.

**Igniting**: On attack rolls that surpasses the target's defense roll by 2 Successes, this weapon's attack also afflicts the target with the On Fire Condition for Successes Rounds.

**Intimidating**: On attack rolls that surpasses the Target's defense roll by 2 Successes, this weapon's attack also afflicts the target with the Fear Condition.

**Piercing**: Projectiles and Lines fired from this weapon ignore the non-obscuring obstacle Disadvantage and do not stop at the first obstacle they encounter.

**Point-Blank**: This ranged weapon was built for close-quarters combat. Not only does it have no Disadvantage on shooting a projectile on adjacent targets, it also deals 2 extra Damage to them.

**Poisoning**: On Criticals, this weapon's attack also afflicts the target with the Poisoned Condition.

**Pulling**: On attack rolls that surpasses the Target's defense roll by 2 Successes, the Target is pulled up to 4 Gridspaces towards the User, granted they could feasibly move at said direction in a straight line.

**Puncturing**: On attack rolls that surpasses the Target's defense roll by 2 Successes, this weapon's projectile punches through the Target, granting them a Projectile Hole Injury, and continues its trajectory as if the first target it hit wasn't there.

**Pushing**: On attack rolls that surpasses the Target's defense roll by 2 Successes, the Target is pushed up to 4 Gridspaces chosen by the User, granted they could feasibly move at said direction in a straight line.

**Radiant**: This weapon deals 2 extra Radiant Damage to Creatures classified as Undead, Aberrations, and other such classifications on top of their normal damage.

**Siege Weapon**: This weapon deals four times the damage to Creatures, double damage to Vehicles, and full damage to Structures.

**Straight Path**: Projectiles shot from this weapon hardly deviate from their trajectory. When shooting against a target that has not moved this Round, gain Advantage 2.

**Stunning**: On Criticals, this weapon's attack also afflicts the target with the Dazed Condition for Successes + 2 Rounds.

**Tripping**: On attack rolls that surpasses the Target's defense roll by 2 Successes, this weapon's attack also afflicts the target with the Prone Condition.

# Armor Keywords
The Keywords pertaining to Armor. It inherits all the Keywords in the General Equipment category.

### Armor Notable Keywords
**Base Armor**: This armor is not an actual item, it is instead the baseline stats for this kind of armor. To turn it into an actual item, add the Keywords between () from the appropriate materials to this Base Armor's Keywords.

**Dodge Penalty -X**: Reduce the user's Dodge attribute by the listed amount. The minimum Dodge a Creature can have is 1.

**Shielding Power X**: This armor adds X amount of Shielding to the defense roll. For example, an armor with Shielding Power 2 will subtract two extra damage when rolling defense.

**Utility Space X**: The amount of Utilities this armor can hold.

### Armor General Keywords
**Climbing Gear**: While wearing this armor, the user's Climbing Speed is the same as their Walking Speed.

**Comfy**: This suit of armor reduces Exposure Damage taken by 1 point.

**Concealed Armor**: This suit of armor is tailored to look similar to day-to-day clothing. Tests to detect that this armor is actually armor have Disadvantage 2.

**Flexible**: This suit of armor is made out of flexible material. Dodge Rolls made while wearing this suit of armor have 1 Mishap re-rolled.

**Flying Harness**: While wearing this armor, the user's Flying Speed is the same as their Walking Speed.

**Waterbound**: While wearing this armor, the user's Swimming Speed is the same as their Walking Speed.

# Utility Keywords
The Keywords pertaining to Utility. It inherits all the Keywords in the General Equipment and Armor categories.

### Utility Notable Keywords
**Utility**: Marks the size of an utility and, as a result, the amount of Utility Space it takes. The Utility Space used is dictated by the modifier on the Utility Keyword:
- Utility: This utility take 1 Utility Space;
- Large Utility: This utility take 2 Utility Spaces;
- Huge Utility: This utility take 3 Utility Spaces.

### Utility General Keywords
**Depth Striding**: While wearing this Utility the user is able to move at the bottom of liquid pools with their full Walking Speed.

**Protection, Cold**: This utility makes the user immune to the effects of Cold weather.

**Protection, Freezing**: This utility makes the user immune to the effects of Cold and Freezing weather.

**Protection, Heat**: This utility makes the user immune to the effects of Hot weather.

**Protection, Melting**: This utility makes the user immune to the effects of Hot and Melting weather.
